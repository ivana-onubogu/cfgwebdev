About Me, or Luvvacation



MY ROOTS GALLERY

because we not dead!

Welcome to the About Me page, inspired by the luvvacation visible in the first image of my Roots gallery. In the front matter of Arthur Pfister's poetry collection, Beer Cans, Bullets, Things & Pieces, he describes a dedication that is actually a 'liv' or 'luv'-ication, because he rejects the phonetic sound of 'dead' that begins dedication. I loved that/it struck me and so I'm borrowing it here.

You can find a series of photos that I feel best represents me, my values and my interests. This is a living gallery; you can hover over images to get a short description and you can expect that more images will populate over time.



h3> FAVORITE CULTURAL FOODS


Nigerian fufu

Nigerian fufu is a traditional dish that usually features a hand-pounded starch (such as yam or cassava) and an accompanying stew. Pictured above you'll see my favorite combination: pounded yam and egusi stew. Growing up, this was eaten both for regular dinners/lunches and at special events. If you click on the image, you can watch a video of it being made. When I was younger, I enjoyed bitterleaf stew the most; though I still enjoy it, egusi has risen to the top two spots (and it ain't two!). I hope that if you haven't tried, you could add it to to your food bucket list.

Southern fried catfish

Southern fried catfish is exactly what it sounds like, although perfecting it is maybe not so easy to discern. If you click on the image you can watch a video and give it a try yourself. While both my parents are Nigerian, I have only ever lived here in America and understand my cultural to be as 'truly' African-American as it is Nigerian. So, fried catfish has got to be that favorite cultural dish of mine. As a kid, it was hanging out with friends that put me on. I haven't looked back since.
